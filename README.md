# Silver Screens Moneyball
A personal project by Jordan Dennis

## Summary
How could you possibly quantify art? Ever since the early 1900s, brave men and women have graced the silver screen. From Charlie Chaplin's black and white slapstick comedy to today's blockbuster, CGI enhanced goliath films, the history of cinema is storied and always changing. The ever present question is, with so many variables and each film being so deeply personal to the viewer, how can you quantify the human aspect of the industry? Certainly, you can rely on Steven Spielberg to make a film worth watching. Does it really matter who plays the lead role, or is a great director worth his weight in gold? These are the questions this projects hopes to answer as we dig through the massive amounts of data the industry has already created. Not only will we accummulate earnings to spendings, but cut across differences in eras, budgets, and opportunity cost as we attempt to quantify the contributions of the thousands that have played their part on the big screen.

## Goal
At the end of the project, the hope is to be able to attribute to actors and directors a number of figures:
- How their presence in a given film effects the critical reception
- The net outcome on film revenue with respect to an individual's salary
- Outside of critical response, how well is an individual received by the general public
Aside from simply tallying totals and adjusting for inflation, the goal is to normalize the data to reflect the market at the time the film was made. This is reminiscent of baseball's WAR statistic, pioneered by Sean Smith in 2010, whose work still serves as the foundation of one of baseball's most exciting and total measurement's of a player's skill today.

## Data
IMDB holds the definitive database for films, yet accessing the information has been found to be cost prohibitive. As such, the data will be found from a number of different sources and combined. First and foremost comes credits. We need to create a significant database of movies with full credits. If at all possible, it would be nice to inlcude behind the scenes members for films, producers, audio and visual effects specialists, scoring, costume desginers, and so on and so forth. Every project contains unsung hereos, and those that excel in their field should be recognized for their exceptional contributions.
